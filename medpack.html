Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before signing up.

Is MedPack free on the NHS?

Yes. If you receive your medications on an NHS prescription, the MedPack service itself is free of charge. You pay the standard NHS prescription charge per item (or nothing if you are exempt) — the pouch dispensing service has no additional cost.

Which medications can go in the pouches?

Most solid oral medications — tablets and capsules — can be placed in MedPack pouches. Some medications cannot be pouched for safety or stability reasons (for example, certain liquids, inhalers, or items requiring refrigeration). Our pharmacy team will advise you on anything that needs to stay in its original packaging.

How do I sign up?

Simply visit your nearest Nigel Williams Pharmacy branch and speak to a member of our team, or click the Sign Up button below to get in touch. We will take your details, confirm your medications with your GP, and get your first MedPack ready for you.

How long does it take to set up?

Once we have received your prescription and confirmed the details with your GP, most patients receive their first MedPack within 5–7 working days. After that, your repeat supply is ready whenever your prescription is due.

Can I still use MedPack if some medications cannot go in the pouches?

Absolutely. Items that cannot be pouched — such as inhalers, liquids, or creams — are dispensed separately alongside your MedPack at the same time, so everything comes together.

What if my medication changes?

Just let us know. Our team will update your MedPack as soon as your GP issues the updated prescription. You will never receive an outdated pouch — our team checks your current medication list every cycle.

Ready to simplify your medications?

Speak to our friendly team in store, or get in touch online and we will get you set up with MedPack — completely free.
